## 🔐 General Digital Safety

**1. Use strong, unique passwords for every account.**
Create passwords with at least 12 characters combining uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols (e.g., R@n1t#Vit2025). Never reuse a password across two platforms. Use a free password manager like Bitwarden to keep track securely.

**2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) everywhere.**
Turn on 2FA on your email, social media, GitHub, LinkedIn, and banking apps. Even if someone learns your password, they cannot log in without the second code sent to your phone.

**3. Keep all software, apps, and your operating system updated.**
Updates patch known security vulnerabilities. Enable automatic updates on your smartphone and laptop. Outdated apps are the most common entry point for malware.

**4. Think before clicking any link.**
If an SMS, email, or WhatsApp message contains a link asking you to "verify," "claim," or "activate" something, do not click it directly. Go to the official website by typing the URL yourself.

**5. Verify identity before trusting any caller or message.**
If someone contacts you claiming to be from your bank, VIT administration, or a government body, hang up and call the official number listed on that organisation's official website to verify.

**6. Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks.**
Do not log into bank accounts, UPI apps, or college portals on public Wi-Fi (cafes, buses, airports). Use your mobile data or a trusted VPN for any task involving passwords or payments.

## 💰 Financial / UPI Safety

**7. Receiving money NEVER requires your UPI PIN.**
This is the single most important rule. In UPI, you only enter your PIN to *send* money — never to *receive* it. If any person, message, or "portal" asks you to enter your PIN or scan a QR code to receive a scholarship, refund, or prize — it is a scam. Disconnect immediately and block the contact.

**8. Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, or CVV with anyone — ever.**
No bank official, university administrator, government representative, or technical support agent has any legitimate reason to ask for these credentials. Not even your own bank's call centre. If anyone asks — it is fraud.

**9. Verify the recipient name before confirming any UPI payment.**
After entering a UPI ID or scanning a QR code, your app will display the registered account name. Always verify this name before entering your PIN. Fraudsters create UPI IDs that look nearly identical to legitimate ones (e.g., vitbhopal.scholarship@upi vs. vitbhopall.scholarship@upi).

**10. Set a daily UPI transaction limit on your banking app.**
Most bank apps allow you to cap your daily UPI outflow at a lower amount (e.g., Rs. 2,000–5,000). Reducing this limit minimises potential losses if your phone or PIN is ever compromised.

## 🚨 How to Report Cybercrime in India

If you or someone you know becomes a victim of cybercrime:

| Channel | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — file a complaint online 24/7 |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** — call immediately for financial fraud; faster recovery in first 24 hours |
| **Local Police Station** | File an FIR for serious cases (identity theft, threats, large financial fraud) |
| **Your Bank** | Call your bank's 24/7 helpline to freeze transactions as soon as fraud is suspected |

> ⚡ **Act within the first hour.** In UPI fraud cases, the sooner you report to your bank and call 1930, the higher the probability that the money can be frozen before being withdrawn.
