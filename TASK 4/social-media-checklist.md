# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

**Prepared by:** Ranit Banerjee | Reg No.: 25MIP10012
**Dept:** Int. M.Tech – Data Science and Computational Intelligence | VIT Bhopal University | CSE0001

---

## ✅ DO's

1. **Think before every post.** Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or a future employer saw this?" If the answer is no, don't post it. Digital content is permanent even after deletion.

2. **Keep all profiles professional.** Use a clear, appropriate profile photo on LinkedIn and GitHub. Write your bio accurately — your name, field of study, and what you are working on.

3. **Share your academic and creative work.** Post about completed assignments, certifications, projects, and events you attended. This builds a credible digital reputation over time — something recruiters actively look for.

4. **Engage respectfully in online discussions.** Comment constructively on others' posts, credit ideas you borrow, and avoid heated public arguments. Disagreement is fine; disrespect is not.

5. **Use privacy settings actively.** Restrict personal content — family photos, home location, daily routine — to close connections only. Review your privacy settings on each platform at least once a semester.

6. **Follow industry leaders, researchers, and communities.** LinkedIn and Twitter/X are excellent for staying updated on Data Science trends, job openings, and research breakthroughs in your field.

7. **Report and block abusive or fraudulent accounts.** Use platform reporting tools if you encounter harassment, impersonation, fake scholarship offers, or any scam-like behaviour.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't share personal information publicly.** Never post your phone number, home address, daily schedule, registration number, or financial details on any public post or open group.

2. **Don't spread unverified news or statistics.** Always verify the source before sharing. A misleading post shared by a student can spread to hundreds of peers instantly. Check before you click Share.

3. **Don't post or amplify offensive content.** Memes, jokes, or comments that demean any person, religion, caste, gender, or community can result in serious disciplinary and legal consequences under Indian IT law.

4. **Don't share your location in real-time.** Posting "at airport, leaving for Delhi" or tagging your live location in a public story is a security risk. Share travel memories after you return.

5. **Don't respond to strangers asking for favours, money, or credentials.** Any request for your OTP, UPI PIN, bank details, or "verification code" from an unknown account — regardless of how official it looks — is a scam. Block and report.

6. **Don't use "Sign in with Facebook/Google" carelessly on unverified websites.** This grants third parties access to your account data. Only use OAuth login on well-known, trusted platforms.

7. **Don't criticise your institution, faculty, or peers publicly.** Screenshots of venting posts have led to disciplinary hearings for students at multiple Indian universities. Address genuine concerns through official email or feedback forms instead.
